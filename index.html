Vision

Neuromorphic Inference Lab is a long-term research initiative exploring how brain-inspired architectures and spiking neural networks (SNNs) can support autonomous agents that reason and act under uncertainty.

The project focuses on:

  • Neuromorphic hardware and realistic simulators
  • Probabilistic and active-inference–based algorithms
  • Methods for using neuromorphic processors as first-class computational substrates for learning, prediction and decision-making

High-level roadmap

  • Phase 0 – Foundations: Build core understanding of SNNs, generative models, and active inference.
  • Phase 1 – Simulated agents: Implement simple SNN-based agents in toy environments.
  • Phase 2 – Active inference on SNNs: Prototype active-inference–style agents with spiking architectures.
  • Phase 3 – Towards hardware: Explore deployment on neuromorphic platforms or realistic simulators.
  • Phase 4 – Research outputs: Consolidate tools and experiments into research-grade artefacts.
